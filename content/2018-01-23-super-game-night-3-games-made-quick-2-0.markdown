This probably doesn't work too well as a simulator for solving the _general_ problem â€” you'd have to find an optimal solution for _every_ permutation of N pancakes to be sure you were right.  But it's a nice interactive illustration of the problem, and if you know the pancake number for your stack size of choice (which I wish the game told you â€” for seven pancakes, it's 8), then trying to restore a stack in that many moves makes for a nice quick puzzle.

The concept here was to _kill the frames, save the animals_, which is a delightfully literal riff on a long-running AGDQ/SGDQ donation incentive â€” people vote with their dollars to decide whether Super Metroid speedrunners go out of their way to free the critters who show you how to walljump and shinespark. The trouble isâ€¦  the marble's movement is always relative to _the world_, **not the camera**.  That means if you turn the camera 30Â° and then try to move the marble, it'll move at a 30Â° angle from your point of view.
